Bio

Born 1988, raised in Gdynia, Poland. I am a PhD Biologist with fair share of neurophysiological procedures on living (and surviving!) animals, laboratory classes and few EEG workshops. Nowadays, I work in back end validation and lately development, mostly around OS-level tools and platform drivers. I was involved in Optane Memory project, a validation paradigm shift-left change for server platform drivers and currently I'm starting my journey as memory reference code developer. In the meantime I got to know the inner workings of an internal startup. All four switches were within the same company and at this point I don't know if I am developing products, or do the products develop me. I guess either way is right. In the future I'd like to move towards Edge devices in biomedical-related fields, and dip my toes in technical writing.

Senior Technical Referent - 2014-2017 - University of Gdańsk

I was responsible for designing and conducting procedures on living laboratory rats - intracranial implantations of EEG recording electrodes and injection cannulas, rat conditioning, handling, signal recording and post processing - all these things were also part of my PhD studies. I applied for grants and received internal one for project named "Parabrachial nucleus and its potential influence on the regulation of theta rhythm in rats". Preliminary results indicated a trend but it was not continued. If you wish to continue that, remember that you are getting into angled implantation (a large blood vessel in the way) and it's far away from the bregma point. You'd need to start from a point much closer to the nuclei to mitigate at least some developmental differences between the animals. As part of my duties I also prepared laboratory space for student classes during the semester. I led neuroanatomy, anatomy and physiology lab classes during my studies and co-authored few publications.

- Secured internal research grant for Parabrachial nucleus and its potential influence on the regulation of theta rhythm in rats - Led neurophysiology, physiology, and anatomy laboratories - Led practical Electroencephalography (EEG) workshops for external listeners - Co-authored 3 major publications in the field of animal neurophysiology
